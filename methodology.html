Methodology

Methodology and limitations

Last updated: 2026-03-30

Important. Charts and estimated views in health-related apps can be easy to over-read. Any estimate shown by Medensa is non-clinical, informational, and subject to important limitations.

1. Purpose of this page

This page explains, at a high level, how Medensa generates trend charts, summaries, reminder logic, and estimated medication level views.

2. Inputs used by Medensa

  • medication and routine details selected by the user;
  • dose amount, date, and time entered by the user;
  • historical logs maintained by the user;
  • optional imported weight history and height where Apple Health has been connected;
  • generalised assumptions used to generate estimated views.

3. Outputs that may be shown

Depending on the feature and plan, Medensa may display timelines, weekly summaries, adherence views, reminder histories, streaks, trend charts, weight views, symptom summaries, goal progress, and estimated medication level views.

4. Key limitations

  • An estimated medication level view is not a direct measurement of medication in the body.
  • It is not a serum concentration reading, blood test, or laboratory result.
  • It does not account for all individual factors that may affect absorption, metabolism, or response.
  • Medensa does not validate the medical accuracy of user-entered data.
  • Missed doses, partial doses, clinician-specific instructions, and unusual treatment plans may not be fully reflected unless they are accurately recorded and supported by product logic.

5. What these outputs must not be used for

You must not use charts, summaries, reminders, or estimated views generated by Medensa to make medication, treatment, or diagnosis decisions.

6. How the estimate should be understood

The estimated medication level view is generated from dose timing and simplified decay assumptions. It exists to support personal review of your own records, not to predict clinical effect or tell you what to do next.

7. Changes over time

If Medensa materially changes the way it generates user-facing visualisations or estimates, this page may be updated to reflect the revised approach.
