Methodology

Methodology and limitations

Last updated: 2026-03-30

Important. Estimated medication level for personal tracking only. Not medical advice.

1. Purpose of this page

This page explains, at a high level, how Medensa builds reminders, charts, summaries, and estimated medication level views from the information available in the app.

2. Inputs Medensa can use

  • medication and routine details selected by the user;
  • dose amount, date, and time entered by the user;
  • weight, symptom, and other routine history kept in the app;
  • optional Apple Health weight history and height, if Apple Health has been connected;
  • generalized assumptions used for estimated views.

3. Outputs Medensa can show

Depending on the feature and plan, Medensa may show reminders, timelines, weight views, history summaries, calendar review, widgets, and estimated medication level.

4. How the estimated medication level is built

The estimated medication level view is based on the dose history recorded in the app plus simplified timing and decay assumptions. It is designed to provide a stable personal-tracking view of your own records rather than a direct measurement.

5. Important limitations

  • An estimated medication level is not a blood test, lab result, or direct reading.
  • It does not capture every factor that may affect absorption, metabolism, or day-to-day experience.
  • Missed or incomplete records can change the output.
  • Reminder timing and summaries also depend on device settings, permissions, and the information available in the app.

6. How to understand charts and summaries

Charts and summaries may help you notice changes in your own records over time, but they should be treated as personal tracking views only.

7. Medical decisions

Always check with your clinician before making medical decisions.

8. Updates

If Medensa materially changes how it generates reminders, charts, or estimates, this page may be updated to reflect the revised approach.
